COURSE DESCRIPTION
This course provides students with foundational skills in business analytics and data-driven decision making. Students will learn to analyze business data, create visualizations, and apply statistical methods to solve real-world business problems. The course emphasizes practical application using Excel and introduces basic statistical concepts essential for modern business practice.

COURSE LEARNING OUTCOMES
By the end of this course, students will be able to:

1. Analyze business scenarios using quantitative models and data visualization techniques
2. Apply descriptive statistics to interpret business data and support decision-making
3. Evaluate relationships between business variables using correlation and regression analysis
4. Design data-driven solutions to business problems using Excel tools and functions
5. Communicate analytical findings effectively through charts, dashboards, and written reports

COURSE SCHEDULE AND TOPICS

Module 1: Excel Foundations and Data Management (Weeks 1-2)
This module introduces students to Excel as a business analytics tool. Students will learn essential functions, data organization principles, and best practices for working with business data.

Topics:
- Excel interface and navigation
- Cell references (absolute vs relative)
- Essential functions (SUM, AVERAGE, COUNT, IF)
- Data formatting and validation
- Sorting and filtering data
- Creating professional tables

Module 2: Functions and Business Models (Weeks 3-4)
Building on Excel basics, students will learn to model business scenarios using mathematical functions and logical operators.

Topics:
- Linear functions and break-even analysis
- Quadratic functions for cost modeling
- Logical functions (IF, AND, OR, nested IFs)
- VLOOKUP and XLOOKUP for data retrieval
- Financial functions (NPV, IRR, PMT)

Module 3: Data Visualization (Weeks 5-6)
Students will learn to create effective visualizations that communicate insights and support business decisions.

Topics:
- Chart types and when to use them
- Column, bar, and line charts
- Pie charts and their limitations
- Scatter plots for relationships
- Combination charts
- Dashboard design principles
- Conditional formatting for visual insights

Module 4: Descriptive Statistics (Weeks 7-8)
This module covers statistical measures used to summarize and describe business data.

Topics:
- Measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode)
- Measures of spread (range, variance, standard deviation)
- Percentiles and quartiles
- Distribution shapes and outliers
- Using Excel for statistical analysis
- Interpreting statistical summaries

Module 5: Probability and Business Applications (Weeks 9-10)
Students explore probability concepts and their application in business risk assessment and decision-making.

Topics:
- Basic probability rules
- Conditional probability
- Expected value and decision trees
- Discrete probability distributions
- Normal distribution basics
- Using probability for business decisions

Module 6: Correlation and Regression (Weeks 11-12)
The final module introduces students to analyzing relationships between variables using correlation and regression analysis.

Topics:
- Scatter plots and correlation
- Correlation coefficient (Pearson's r)
- Interpreting correlation strength
- Simple linear regression
- Regression equation and predictions
- Coefficient of determination (RÂ²)
- Limitations and assumptions
